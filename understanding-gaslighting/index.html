"Why Do I Feel Crazy Around My Parent?" Understanding Gaslighting

How narcissistic parents make you doubt your own mind — and how to hold onto your reality.

🌸 Gentle Note: This article discusses gaslighting and psychological manipulation. Reading about these patterns can stir up intense memories or self-doubt. If your mind starts to spin, pause here. Take a step back, look around your room, and name three physical things you can see. Your sanity is real.

Have you ever walked out of an argument with your parent feeling so dizzy and confused that you had to sit down and ask yourself if you are actually losing your mind?

Maybe you confronted them about something painful they said to you last week, and they looked you dead in the eye and said, "I never said that. You have a wild imagination."

This dizzying, foggy feeling isn't a sign that you are losing your memory. It is the signature psychological impact of gaslighting.

What is Gaslighting? (The Non-Fluff Definition)

Gaslighting is a deliberate manipulation tactic used to make you doubt your own memory, perception, and sanity. When a parent gaslights you, they are trying to replace your objective reality with their version of the truth, until you stop trusting your own brain and rely entirely on them.

4 Common Parental Gaslighting Phrases

1. "I never said that. You're making things up."

It attacks your memory. The underlying message: "My self-image is too fragile to admit I made a mistake, so I will make you believe your memory is broken instead."

2. "You're too sensitive. It was just a joke."

It attacks your emotional reality. The message: "I am allowed to hurt you, but you are not allowed to make me feel uncomfortable by showing your pain."

3. "You have a terrible memory."

It builds a long-term narrative that you are unreliable. Once they convince you your brain is "faulty," they can rewrite any event.

4. "After everything I've done for you, how can you say that?"

It uses guilt and obligation to distract. The message: "My basic parental duties buy me the permanent right to treat you however I want."

Why Do Narcissistic Parents Gaslight?

They gaslight for two reasons: Absolute Control — by making you doubt your sanity, they keep you dependent on them for "the truth." And Protecting Their Ego — they cannot handle shame, so they rewrite history to make you the bad guy.

The Hidden Cost

Growing up with constant gaslighting leaves deep scars. You might find yourself apologizing constantly, struggling to make basic decisions, or recording conversations just to prove to yourself you aren't imagining things.

If you are recording fights just to check your own sanity, that is not a sign of your craziness — it is absolute proof of how severely you are being gaslit.

How to Hold Onto Your Reality (3 Safe Strategies)

Strategy 1: Stop Trying to Convince Them (Drop the Rope)

Use "Internal Validation": "I know what happened. I don't need their permission for my memory to be real." Use neutral phrases: "We remember it differently."

Strategy 2: Keep a "Reality Log" (Safely)

Write down dates, what happened, and how you felt. Keep it password-protected. When the FOG rolls in, open your log — it acts as an anchor for your sanity.

Strategy 3: Find a "Reality Check" Friend

Find a trusted friend, counselor, or therapist. Hearing someone say, "That was incredibly unfair, and your reaction makes complete sense," is like breathing fresh air after drowning.

🌿 A 10-Second Sanity Anchor

Press your feet firmly into the floor. Feel the solid ground beneath you.

Rub your palms together until warm. Place them on your chest.

Say to yourself: "My eyes are real. My ears are real. My memory is mine, and I trust myself."

You are not crazy. The mirror they held up was cracked — but you do not have to believe the distortion.
